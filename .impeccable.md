## Design Context

### Users
Phoenix real estate agents, brokers, and investors who need fast, professional listing media. They are busy professionals — often booking between showings on a phone or laptop. They need to quickly understand what's offered, trust the quality, and book without friction. Many are not tech-savvy; the interface must be immediately obvious. Primary conversion goal: 2 photoshoots booked per day.

### Brand Personality
**Warm · Premium · Local**
Colin is a solo Phoenix operator — approachable and personable (introduces himself by first name), but the work is high-end. The tone sits between "trusted local expert" and "refined boutique." Not corporate. Not cheap. Think: a skilled craftsman who happens to have excellent taste.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Light mode only** — white-dominant, clean, easy on the eyes. Avoid dark backgrounds and black-heavy themes.
- **Gold accent (#C9973A)** — the signature brand color, used for CTAs and highlights. Keep it.
- **Warm off-white base (#FAFAF9)** — not pure white, slightly warm.
- **Competitor to avoid**: desertlens.net — too boring, confusing, corporate.
- **Goal**: So plain and simple ANYONE could use it, but still modern and sleek. No jargon, no confusion.
- **Content priority**: Hero visuals (best photos + videos) → social proof → pricing → booking. Show the work first, sell second.
- Typography: Cormorant Garamond (display) + Inter (body) — already in place, keep this pairing.
- The pricing section with service-type bubbles + package cards is a deliberate UX feature Colin loves — full transparency, no hidden costs. Protect and polish this pattern.

### Design Principles
1. **Show before you tell** — lead with stunning visuals; copy and pricing follow after trust is established
2. **One obvious next step** — every section should funnel toward booking; never leave the user wondering what to do
3. **Plain enough for anyone** — no jargon, no ambiguous labels, no "clever" copy that confuses; clarity wins over cleverness
4. **Local and human** — Colin is a real person in Phoenix; the site should feel like it was made for this market, not a generic template
5. **White-space as luxury** — generous breathing room signals premium quality; don't crowd the page
